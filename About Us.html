About Us

Established in 2018, Dignity4Girls is committed to restoring dignity and confidence to young girls across South Africa. We provide access to sanitary pads, menstrual health education, and community support programs that empower girls to thrive.

Turning Interest Into Action

At Dignity4Girls, we transform curiosity and compassion into meaningful initiatives. Our programs encourage girls to participate in community outreach, educational workshops, and volunteering opportunities that empower them to take action and inspire change.

  • Engage in local community programs
  • Participate in educational workshops
  • Develop leadership and advocacy skills
  • Support peers through mentorship and outreach

Why It Matters

Millions of girls in South Africa face educational setbacks due to period poverty. Without access to sanitary pads, girls miss school and lose opportunities for growth. Dignity4Girls exists to eliminate these barriers and ensure every girl can reach her full potential.

What We Do

Our programs focus on both immediate support and long-term empowerment:

  • Distribution of sanitary pads to schools and vulnerable communities.
  • Workshops on menstrual health education and hygiene practices.
  • Volunteer training and mentorship programs.
  • Advocacy campaigns to break taboos around menstruation.

Our Vision

A world where every girl can manage her menstruation with confidence, dignity, and equality. We strive to empower young girls to thrive academically, socially, and emotionally, without the limitations imposed by period poverty.

Meet Our Founder & CEO

Mbali Mbhele, founder and CEO of Dignity4Girls, established the organization in 2018 to address period poverty and empower young girls across South Africa. Her vision is to create a society where no girl is left behind because of menstruation.

Under her leadership, Dignity4Girls has grown into a trusted organization, delivering vital resources, education, and advocacy programs. Mbali's dedication ensures that every girl has the opportunity to thrive, learn, and participate fully in her community.
